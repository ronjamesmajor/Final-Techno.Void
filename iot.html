The InterWeB of Thangz


Maybe we'll all learn something together.

In today’s modern web, there are so many devices which are interconnected. From your watch to your car to your treat-dispensing-dog-camera to your fridge and your slow cooker. Here are our picks for the best (or worst, depending on your viewpoint) IoT Devices in 2022!

  1. Apple Watch

    The Apple Watch is one of the first IoT devices that we here at Techno Void consider to be truly useful. It can control the lights in your home, it can monitor your sleep. It can set alarms, stopwatches, schedule events, check your bank information, pay for things with Apple Pay, read your heart rate, and even call 911 if it detects you have taken a hard fall and may be hurt. It’s great for fitness enthusiasts and also for tech savvy individuals, but it’s also an overall time saver and convenient device to use if you are a connected person. The only downside? Apple aggregates and collects all of this personal data and builds a profile of you that is stored on their servers, and Apple isn’t telling what they’ll do with that information.


  2. Smart Thermostats

    Of course Google would find a way to aggregate the goings on inside of your home by stealing temperature metrics, the times you are home, or travelling... But is this thing actually functional? Well, this thermostat, while having a gross up front cost of $300-$500, can actually save you money long term. Sure, it collects and profiles the daylights out of you, but it also can allow your furnace or air conditioning unit to work on predefined schedules, and routines, which will almost certainly save you more money than a not-smart programmable digital thermostat....right?


  3. Connected Cars

    Our Techno Void pick for “most disruptive new IoT Technology” goes to Connected Cars. Ever wish that your car could have heated mirrors? What about air conditioned seats? Automated driving? How about the ability to run full system diagnostics, and give you detailed reports on everything about the vehicle? What if I told you that cars already have that technology built in, it’s just turned off, and you have to pay the manufacturer or dealership a monthly subscription fee to access it! Doesn’t that sound a lot better? Of course it does! People love paying subscriptions for products that they already own, especially when there’s an added bonus of every single one of those metrics including geo-location being sent to a data collection company such as Meta or Google.
